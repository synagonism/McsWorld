McsDirTchInfMisc.multiple-of--unit-of-measurement

description::
· unit-multiples are NUMBERS, multiples of 10 or 2, which like all numbers we use to MEASURE (bit, byte, pixel, ANYTHING):
K (kilo) = 1,0001 (103 = 1,000),
M (mega) = 1,0002 (106 = 1,000,000),
G (giga) = 1,0003 (109 = 1 billion),
T (tera) = 1,0004 (1012= 1 trillion),
P (peta) = 1,0005 (1015),
E (exa) = 1,0006 (1018),
Z (zetta) = 1,0007 (1021),
Y (yotta) = 1,0008 (1024),

· in {1998}, the-International-Electrotechnical-Commission-(IEC) established SIMILAR numbers, but multiples of 2 (210=1,024) and not of 10, to use in digital|binary-information where everything is-represented with 2 digits:
Ki (kibi) = 1,0241 (210 = 1,024),
Mi (mebi) = 1,0242 (220 = 1,048,576),
Gi (gibi) = 1,0243 (230 ≆ 1 billion),
Ti (tebi) = 1,0244 (240 ≆ 1 trillion),
Pi (pebi) = 1,0245 (250),
Ei (exbi) = 1,0246 (260),
Zi (zebi) = 1,0247 (270),
Yi (yobi) = 1,0248 (280).
