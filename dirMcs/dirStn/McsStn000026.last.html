* **Import and export volumes and values:** This data shows the quantity and monetary value of goods and services that a country imports and exports.
* **Trade balance:** This measure indicates whether a country is running a trade surplus (exporting more than it imports) or a trade deficit (importing more than it exports).
* **Share of trade in GDP:** This metric shows the proportion of a country's gross domestic product (GDP) that is generated by trade.
* **Import and export composition by sector:** This data breaks down trade by industry, such as agriculture, manufacturing, and services.
* **Top trading partners:** This information identifies the countries with which a country conducts the most trade.

* **World Trade Organization (WTO):** The WTO provides comprehensive trade statistics for WTO members and other major trading partners.
* **International Monetary Fund (IMF):** The IMF's World Economic Outlook provides trade statistics for all countries.
* **National statistical agencies:** Many countries have their own statistical agencies that collect and publish trade data.
* **Trade data providers:** Private companies offer trade data subscription services that provide more detailed and up-to-date information.
**Uses of trade-sector statistics:**
* **Government policy:** Governments use trade-sector statistics to inform trade policy decisions, such as negotiating trade agreements and setting tariffs.
* **Business strategy:** Businesses use trade-sector statistics to identify export opportunities, assess market potential, and make informed investment decisions.
* **Individual decision-making:** Consumers and investors can use trade-sector statistics to understand the impact of trade on prices and economic conditions.

* A government might use trade-sector statistics to identify industries that are exporting at a high rate and provide support to those industries to maintain their competitiveness.
* A business might use trade-sector statistics to assess the market potential for a new product in a foreign market and determine whether to invest in export marketing.
* An investor might use trade-sector statistics to evaluate the economic prospects of a particular country and make informed investment decisions.

* **Mid-range hotels:** These hotels offer more amenities than budget hotels, such as private bathrooms, air conditioning, and a continental breakfast. They are a good option for travelers who want a balance of affordability and comfort.
* **Luxury hotels:** These hotels offer the highest level of luxury and amenities, such as spacious rooms with plush beds, high-end toiletries, and a variety of dining options. They are the most expensive option, but they appeal to travelers who want the best possible experience.

* **Parking:** Parking can be a valuable amenity for travelers who are driving, and many hotels offer free parking for their guests.
