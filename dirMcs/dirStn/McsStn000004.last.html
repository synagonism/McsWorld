description::
">types of administrative-law:
Administrative law is a branch of law that deals with the legal principles and rules governing the administration and regulation of government agencies. It encompasses a wide range of legal issues related to the activities of administrative agencies and their interactions with individuals and businesses. Here are some types of administrative law:

1. **Rulemaking and Regulations:**
- **Legislative Rulemaking:** This involves the process by which administrative agencies create rules and regulations that have the force of law. These rules are often developed to implement and interpret statutes enacted by the legislature.
- **Procedural Rulemaking:** Administrative agencies may also create rules that govern their own procedures, ensuring fairness and transparency in their decision-making processes.

2. **Adjudication:**
- **Administrative Adjudication:** This involves the resolution of disputes between individuals or businesses and government agencies. Administrative law judges within the agencies hear cases, take evidence, and make decisions.

3. **Licensing and Permits:**
- **Licensing:** Administrative agencies often have the authority to issue licenses or permits for various activities, such as professional licenses, business permits, environmental permits, etc.
