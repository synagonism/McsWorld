description::
· the-relations among the-organisms.
===
"Sociality is the degree to which individuals in an animal population tend to associate in social groups (gregariousness) and form cooperative societies.
Sociality is a survival response to evolutionary pressures.[1] For example, when a mother wasp stays near her larvae in the nest, parasites are less likely to eat the larvae.[2] Biologists suspect that pressures from parasites and other predators selected this behavior in wasps of the family Vespidae.
This wasp behaviour evidences the most fundamental characteristic of animal sociality: parental investment. Parental investment is any expenditure of resources (time, energy, social capital) to benefit one's offspring. Parental investment detracts from a parent's capacity to invest in future reproduction and aid to kin (including other offspring). An animal that cares for its young but shows no other sociality traits is said to be subsocial.
An animal that exhibits a high degree of sociality is called a social animal. The highest degree of sociality recognized by sociobiologists is eusociality. A eusocial taxon is one that exhibits overlapping adult generations, reproductive division of labor, cooperative care of young, and—in the most refined cases—a biological caste system."

A parasocial relationship is a one-sided emotional attachment that a person forms with a non-interactive media persona, such as a fictional character, a celebrity, or a social media influencer. The term was coined by sociologist Donald Horton and psychologist Richard Wohl in their 1956 paper "Mass Communication and Parasocial Interaction: Observations on Intimacy at a Distance."

**Examples of Parasocial Relationships**
* A fan who feels like they know a celebrity intimately from watching their interviews and social media posts.
* A child who feels like their favorite cartoon character is their friend.
* A listener who feels like they have a personal connection with a radio or podcast host.

**Causes of Parasocial Relationships**
* **The illusion of intimacy.** Media personas provide a constant stream of information about themselves, including their thoughts, feelings, and experiences. This can create the illusion of intimacy, even though the relationship is not reciprocal.
* **The desire for connection.** People who are lonely or isolated may be more likely to form parasocial relationships, as they can provide a sense of companionship and belonging.
* **The vicarious experience of emotions.** Media personas can provide a safe and controlled way to experience a wide range of emotions, such as love, sadness, and excitement.

**Benefits and Risks of Parasocial Relationships**
**Benefits:**
* **Social connection and companionship.** Parasocial relationships can provide a sense of connection and companionship, even for people who are lonely or isolated.
* **Emotional support.** Media personas can provide a listening ear and emotional support, especially for people who are going through difficult times.
* **Self-esteem and identity.** Parasocial relationships can help people to develop their self-esteem and identity by providing role models and inspiration.

**Risks:**
* **Disillusionment.** When people realize that their parasocial relationships are not real, they can experience feelings of disappointment, sadness, and anger.
* **Addiction.** People who are addicted to parasocial relationships may spend excessive amounts of time and energy engaging with media personas, to the detriment of their other relationships and responsibilities.
* **Social anxiety.** Parasocial relationships may make people more anxious about forming real-world relationships, as they may fear that they will not be able to live up to the idealized images of their media personas.

**Overall, parasocial relationships are a complex phenomenon with both positive and negative aspects.** While they can provide a sense of connection and comfort, they are not a substitute for real-world relationships. It is important for people to be aware of the potential risks of parasocial relationships and to maintain a healthy balance between their online and offline interactions."
