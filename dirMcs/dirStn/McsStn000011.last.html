Employment job attributes, also known as job characteristics, are the factors that make a job appealing or unattractive to potential employees. These attributes can be categorized into two main groups:

**Hard attributes** are objective and quantifiable qualities related to the job itself, such as:
* **Salary:** The amount of money paid for the job.
* **Benefits:** The non-monetary rewards offered by the employer, such as health insurance, retirement savings plans, and paid time off.
* **Job security:** The likelihood of the job being eliminated or downsized.
* **Work-life balance:** The ability to balance work demands with personal life commitments.
* **Opportunities for advancement:** The potential for promotion and career growth within the company.
* **Training and development:** The opportunities for employees to learn new skills and advance their careers.
* **Physical demands:** The physical exertion required for the job.
* **Skills and experience required:** The specific skills and experience needed to perform the job duties.

**Soft attributes** are subjective and qualitative qualities related to the work environment and company culture, such as:
* **Company culture:** The overall atmosphere and values of the company.
* **Management style:** The leadership approach of the managers and supervisors.

They do not own the means of production (e.g., factories, machinery, or tools) and are not self-employed.

**Key characteristics of wage workers:**
* **Sell their labor power:** Wage workers sell their time and skills to an employer in exchange for a wage.
* **No ownership of means of production:** Wage workers do not own the tools, machinery, or other resources used to produce goods or services.
* **Dependence on an employer:** Wage workers rely on their employer for their livelihood.
* **Hierarchical relationship:** Wage workers are typically subordinate to their employers in a hierarchical organizational structure.
* **Wage as the primary source of income:** Wage workers typically earn their primary income from their wages.
* **Limited control over work:** Wage workers typically have limited control over their work, including their tasks, hours, and working conditions.

**Types of wage workers**
Wage workers can be categorized into different types based on their job characteristics, skills, and level of education. Some common types of wage workers include:
* **Factory workers:** Engage in tasks related to manufacturing and production.
* **Service workers:** Provide services to customers, such as retail workers, hospitality workers, and healthcare professionals.
* **Professionals:** Possess specialized skills and knowledge, such as doctors, engineers, lawyers, and teachers.
* **Managers:** Supervise and coordinate the work of other employees.

**Impact of technology on wage workers**
Technology has had a significant impact on the lives of wage workers, both positive and negative.

**Positive impacts:**
* **Increased productivity:** Technology has enabled businesses to automate tasks and improve efficiency, leading to increased productivity.
* **New employment opportunities:** Technology has created new employment opportunities in fields such as software development, data analysis, and artificial intelligence.
* **Improved working conditions:** Technology can help to improve working conditions by reducing the physical demands of work and increasing safety.

**Negative impacts:**
* **Job displacement:** Technology can lead to job displacement as automation replaces certain tasks and roles.
* **Precarious work:** The rise of the gig economy and temporary work arrangements has created more precarious work for some wage workers.
* **Increased worker surveillance:** Technology can be used to monitor and surveil workers, raising concerns about privacy and freedom.
