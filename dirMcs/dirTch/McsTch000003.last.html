- Brushless DC motors, also known as electronically commutated motors (ECMs), gained popularity in the 1960s. These motors eliminated the need for brushes and commutators, resulting in reduced maintenance and improved efficiency.

- Efforts were made to enhance the efficiency of electric motors to address energy conservation concerns. High-efficiency standards and regulations were implemented, driving the development of more energy-efficient motor designs.
